Aviation?

   Even Socrates agrees that, "Man must rise above the Earth—to the top of the atmosphere and beyond—for only thus will he fully understand the world in which he lives." Taking a dip in the world of aviation is akin to learning a transformative new life skill.

   There's plenty of things that will drag you down, but let these be just the start of the list of reasons—the wind beneath your wings—to pursue General Aviation:


3:

It sure beats driving!

The soaring pilot makes an aerial excursion, not an incursion. His passage leaves a whisper, not a shriek.
              ~Richard Miller, 1967

2:

You don't have to put up with the airlines!

   Ever rush to the airport just to find out your flight's been delayed? Or sat next to a screaming baby for a 4 hour flight? When you're in the cockpit, you don't have to deal with any of that!


1:

Flying helps you become a better person.

   Being a proficient pilot requires discipline, planning, and thinking in a whole new way. Flying isn't merely about transportation; it's about transformation. (If this isn't a good enough reason, try this prospect.)



   On behalf of the PPGS instuctors, I invite you into this fascinating, new world of Aviation, a chance to change your perspective on the world...literally.

So what are you waiting for? Hop in!

Once you have tasted flight, you will forever walk the earth with your eyes turned skyward, for there you have been, and there you will always long to return.
              ~Leonardo da Vinci
